# Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)

Source summary covering the systematic investment strategy of investing fixed amounts at regular intervals.

---

## Key Facts

- **Definition**: Investing equal dollar amounts at regular intervals regardless of price
- **Result**: More shares purchased when prices are low, fewer when prices are high
- **Benefit**: Removes emotion and market timing from investment decisions

---

## How It Works

1. Set a fixed investment amount (e.g., $500/month)
2. Choose a regular schedule (weekly, monthly, bi-weekly)
3. Invest the same amount each period regardless of market conditions
4. Average cost per share smooths out over time

---

## Example Calculation

| Month | Price | Shares Bought ($500) |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| Jan   | $50   | 10.00 shares        |
| Feb   | $40   | 12.50 shares        |
| Mar   | $60   | 8.33 shares         |
| **Total** | — | **30.83 shares @ $48.65 avg** |

---

## Implementation Methods

- **401(k) contributions**: Automatic payroll deduction
- **Automatic investing**: Brokerage recurring purchases
- **Dividend reinvestment (DRIP)**: Automated dividend purchases

---

## Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Removes emotional decisions | May underperform lump-sum in rising markets |
| Simple to automate | Requires discipline to maintain |
| Reduces timing risk | Still exposed to overall market risk |
