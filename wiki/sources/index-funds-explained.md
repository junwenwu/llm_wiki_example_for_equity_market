# Index Funds Explained

Source summary covering passive investment vehicles that track market indexes.

---

## Key Facts

- **Definition**: Funds that passively track a market index (S&P 500, Total Market, etc.)
- **Philosophy**: Market is efficient; beating it consistently is difficult and expensive
- **Cost advantage**: Expense ratios 0.03-0.20% vs 0.50-1.50% for active funds

---

## Types of Index Funds

### Mutual Funds
- Minimum investments required (often $1,000-$3,000)
- Trade once daily at NAV
- Examples: VFIAX, FXAIX, SWPPX

### Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)
- No minimums (buy single shares)
- Trade throughout the day like stocks
- Examples: VOO, SPY, VTI, QQQ

---

## Popular Index Categories

| Category | Example Index | Example Fund |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| U.S. Large Cap | S&P 500 | VOO, VFIAX |
| U.S. Total Market | CRSP Total Market | VTI, VTSAX |
| International | FTSE Developed ex-US | VXUS, VTIAX |
| Bonds | Bloomberg Aggregate | BND, VBTLX |

---

## Key Metrics to Compare

- **Expense Ratio**: Annual fee (lower is better)
- **Tracking Error**: How closely fund follows index
- **Fund Size (AUM)**: Larger funds typically more liquid
- **Tax Efficiency**: ETFs generally more tax-efficient
