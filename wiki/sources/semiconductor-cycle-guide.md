# Semiconductor Cycle Guide

Source summary covering the dynamics of semiconductor industry cycles for investment timing.

---

## The Semiconductor Cycle

### Typical Cycle Length
- **Full cycle**: 3-5 years
- **Expansion**: 18-30 months
- **Contraction**: 12-18 months

### Cycle Phases
1. **Trough**: Excess inventory, low utilization, price wars
2. **Recovery**: Inventory normalization, orders increase
3. **Expansion**: Capacity constraints, pricing power
4. **Peak**: Overbuilding begins, double-ordering
5. **Contraction**: Inventory correction, demand softens

---

## Key Indicators

### Leading Indicators
- **Book-to-bill ratio**: Orders vs shipments (>1 = bullish)
- **Fab utilization**: Capacity usage rates
- **Equipment orders**: CapEx signals future capacity

### Coincident Indicators
- **Revenue growth**: Current demand strength
- **Gross margins**: Pricing power/supply tightness
- **Inventory days**: Channel health

### Lagging Indicators
- **Capacity additions**: New fabs coming online
- **Hiring/layoffs**: Employment trends

## Sector Segments

| Segment | Cyclicality | Key Players |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Memory (DRAM/NAND)** | High | MU, Samsung, SK Hynix |
| **Logic/Foundry** | Medium | TSM, INTC, Samsung |
| **Analog** | Low-Medium | TXN, ADI, ON |
| **AI/Data Center** | Lower (structural growth) | NVDA, AMD, AVGO |
