# Three-Fund Portfolio

Source summary covering the Bogleheads' simple, diversified portfolio strategy.

---

## Key Facts

- **Origin**: Bogleheads community (followers of John Bogle, Vanguard founder)
- **Philosophy**: Simplicity, low costs, broad diversification
- **Components**: U.S. stocks + International stocks + U.S. bonds

---

## The Three Funds

| Asset Class | Purpose | Example Funds |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| **U.S. Total Stock** | Domestic equity growth | VTI, VTSAX, FSKAX |
| **International Stock** | Global diversification | VXUS, VTIAX, FTIHX |
| **U.S. Total Bond** | Stability, income | BND, VBTLX, FXNAX |

---

## Sample Allocations

| Age/Risk Profile | U.S. Stock | Intl Stock | Bonds |
|------------------|------------|------------|-------|
| Aggressive (20s-30s) | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| Moderate (40s-50s) | 45% | 25% | 30% |
| Conservative (60s+) | 30% | 15% | 55% |

---

## Rebalancing

- **Frequency**: Annually or when allocation drifts >5%
- **Method**: Sell overweight, buy underweight (or direct new contributions)
- **Tax consideration**: Rebalance in tax-advantaged accounts first

---

## Why It Works

- Captures global market returns at minimal cost
- Simple to maintain (no stock picking required)
- Historically outperforms most active strategies long-term
- Automatic diversification across thousands of securities
