# Earnings Analysis Guide

Source summary providing a comprehensive framework for analyzing company earnings reports.

---

## Key Metrics Framework

### Income Statement Metrics
- **Revenue/Sales**: Top-line growth, organic vs acquired
- **Gross Margin**: Pricing power, cost structure
- **Operating Margin**: Operational efficiency
- **Net Income**: Bottom-line profitability
- **EPS (Diluted)**: Per-share earnings for valuation

### Growth Metrics
- **YoY (Year-over-Year)**: Compare to same quarter last year
- **QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)**: Sequential momentum
- **Guidance**: Management's forward outlook

---

## Beat/Miss Analysis

| Scenario | Market Reaction | Interpretation |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Beat + Raise guidance | Positive | Strong execution, confidence |
| Beat + Lower guidance | Mixed/Negative | One-time factors, concerns ahead |
| Miss + Raise guidance | Mixed | Temporary setback, optimistic |
| Miss + Lower guidance | Negative | Fundamental problems |

---

## Sector-Specific Metrics

### Technology (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN)
- Cloud revenue growth, margins
- User/subscriber metrics
- R&D spending trends

### Semiconductors (NVDA, AMD, INTC)
- Data center vs consumer mix
- Gross margin trends
- Inventory levels, book-to-bill

### Automotive (TSLA)
- Deliveries, production capacity
- ASP (Average Selling Price)
- Energy/services revenue

---

## Earnings Call Analysis

### Key Sections
1. **Prepared remarks**: Management narrative
2. **Q&A session**: Analyst probing, management candor
3. **Forward guidance**: Next quarter/year outlook

### Red Flags
- Vague answers to direct questions
- Blame on "macro" without specifics
- Changing accounting methods
- Executive departures around earnings
