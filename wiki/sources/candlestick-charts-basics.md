# Candlestick Charts Basics

Source summary from Investopedia covering Japanese candlestick charting fundamentals for active traders.

---

## Key Facts

- **Origin**: Japanese rice traders (18th century), popularized by Steve Nison
- **Components**: Real body (open-close range), upper shadow (wick), lower shadow (tail)
- **Color coding**: Green/white = bullish (close > open), Red/black = bearish (close < open)

---

## Core Patterns Covered

### Single-Candle Patterns
- **Doji**: Open = close, signals indecision
- **Hammer**: Small body, long lower shadow, bullish reversal at bottom
- **Inverted Hammer**: Small body, long upper shadow
- **Spinning Top**: Small body, shadows on both sides

### Multi-Candle Patterns
- **Engulfing**: Second candle completely engulfs first (bullish/bearish variants)
- **Harami**: Small candle inside previous large candle ("pregnant" pattern)
- **Morning Star**: Three-candle bullish reversal (down, doji, up)
- **Evening Star**: Three-candle bearish reversal (up, doji, down)

---

## Application Notes

- Best used with other indicators (volume, support/resistance)
- Pattern reliability varies by timeframe
- Confirmation candle recommended before acting
