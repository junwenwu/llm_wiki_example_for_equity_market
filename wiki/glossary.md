## Terminology

### Investing Strategies

**Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)**
: Investing fixed dollar amounts at regular intervals regardless of price. Smooths out cost basis over time.
- See also: [[dollar-cost-averaging]]

**Three-Fund Portfolio**
: Bogleheads strategy using three index funds: U.S. stocks, international stocks, and bonds.
- See also: [[three-fund-portfolio]]

**Passive Investing**
: Strategy of buying and holding index funds to match market returns rather than trying to beat the market.
- See also: [[index-funds-explained]]

### Trading Terms

**Candlestick**
: Chart representation showing open, high, low, and close prices as a "candle" shape with body and shadows.
- See also: [[candlestick-charts-basics]]

**Position Sizing**
: Determining how many shares to buy based on account risk tolerance and trade risk.
- See also: [[position-sizing-risk-management]]

**Stop-Loss**
: Order to sell a security when it reaches a specified price to limit losses.

**2% Rule**
: Risk management guideline to never risk more than 2% of account equity on any single trade.

### Financial Metrics

**EPS (Earnings Per Share)**
: Net income divided by shares outstanding. Key profitability metric.

**P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)**
: Stock price divided by EPS. Valuation metric comparing price to earnings.

**Gross Margin**
: (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue. Measures pricing power and cost efficiency.

**YoY (Year-over-Year)**
: Comparison of a metric to the same period in the prior year.

**QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)**
: Comparison of a metric to the previous quarter.

**ROE (Return on Equity)**
: Net income divided by shareholders' equity. Measures profitability relative to investment.

**TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)**
: Sum of last four quarters of financial data. Used for annualized metrics.

### Semiconductor Terms

**Foundry**
: Semiconductor manufacturing facility that produces chips for other companies.
- Key player: TSM (Taiwan Semiconductor)

**Fabless**
: Semiconductor company that designs chips but outsources manufacturing.
- Examples: NVDA, AMD
- See also: [[semiconductors]]

**HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)**
: Advanced memory technology used in AI accelerators, offering higher bandwidth than standard DRAM.
- Key supplier: [[mu]]

**Book-to-Bill Ratio**
: Orders received divided by orders shipped. Ratio >1 indicates growing demand.

**Utilization Rate**
: Percentage of manufacturing capacity being used. High utilization = pricing power.

**CUDA**
: NVIDIA's parallel computing platform and API. Creates software moat for GPUs.
- See also: [[nvda]]

### Optical/Networking Terms

**Optical Transceiver**
: Device converting electrical signals to optical signals for fiber optic transmission.
- Key players: [[cohr]], [[axti]], [[mxl]]

**800G/1.6T**
: Data transmission speeds (800 Gbps / 1.6 Tbps). Current/next-gen data center interconnect standards.

**InP (Indium Phosphide)**
: Compound semiconductor material used in high-speed optical components.
- Key supplier: [[axti]]

**DSP (Digital Signal Processor)**
: Chip that processes digital signals for optical transceivers.
- Key player: [[mxl]]

**Coherent Optics**
: Technology using phase and amplitude modulation for high-capacity data transmission.

### Healthcare/Telehealth Terms

**Telehealth**
: Healthcare delivery via digital/remote technologies.
- Key player: [[hims]]
- See also: [[healthcare]]

**DTC (Direct-to-Consumer)**
: Business model selling directly to end consumers without intermediaries.

**GLP-1**
: Glucagon-like peptide-1. Drug class used for diabetes and weight loss (Ozempic, Wegovy).
- Relevant to: [[hims]]

**Precision Medicine**
: Medical approach customizing treatment based on individual genetic/molecular profiles.
- Key player: [[tem]]

### Quantum Computing Terms

**Quantum Annealing**
: Quantum computing approach specialized for optimization problems.
- Key player: [[qbts]]
- See also: [[quantum-computing]]

**Qubit**
: Basic unit of quantum information, analogous to classical bit but can exist in superposition.

**Gate-Based Quantum Computing**
: Universal quantum computing using quantum logic gates. Alternative to annealing.

### Index Fund Terms

**Expense Ratio**
: Annual fee charged by a fund, expressed as percentage of assets. Lower is better.

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**
: Per-share value of a fund's holdings. Mutual funds trade at NAV.

**ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**
: Fund that trades on exchanges like a stock, typically tracking an index.

## Style Conventions

| Convention | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Ticker symbols | ALL CAPS, no periods | NVDA, MSFT, VTI |
| Percentages | Use % symbol, one decimal | 65.4%, 2.5% |
| Currency | Dollar sign before, commas | $1,000, $4.82T |
| Large numbers | Use B/M/T suffixes | $68.1B, $4.82T |
| Market cap tiers | Mega (>$500B), Large ($50-500B), Mid/Small (<$50B) | NVDA (Mega), PLTR (Large), HIMS (Small) |
