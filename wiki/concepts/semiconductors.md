# Semiconductors

The semiconductor industry designs and manufactures integrated circuits (chips) that power all electronic devices from smartphones to data centers.

---

## Industry Structure

### Value Chain
1. **Design**: Chip architecture ([[nvda]], [[amd]], QCOM)
2. **Foundry**: Manufacturing (TSM, Samsung, INTC)
3. **Equipment**: Tools for fabs (ASML, AMAT, LRCX)
4. **Memory**: DRAM/NAND ([[mu]], [[sndk]], Samsung, SK Hynix)
5. **Materials**: Substrates ([[axti]], [[glw]])
6. **Packaging/Testing**: Assembly (ASE, Amkor)

### Business Models
- **Fabless**: Design only, outsource manufacturing ([[nvda]], [[amd]])
- **IDM**: Integrated design + manufacturing (INTC, Samsung)
- **Foundry**: Manufacturing for others (TSM)

## Cyclicality

Semiconductors are historically cyclical due to:
- Long capacity build times (2-3 years for new fabs)
- Inventory fluctuations in supply chain
- End-market demand variability

See [[semiconductor-cycle-guide]] for detailed cycle analysis.

---

## AI Impact

The AI boom is transforming semiconductor demand:
- **Data center GPUs**: [[nvda]] dominates with 80%+ share
- **HBM memory**: Severe shortage, premium pricing ([[mu]] key supplier)
- **Custom silicon**: Hyperscalers building own chips (benefit: [[avgo]])
- **Optical bandwidth**: 800G/1.6T transceivers in demand ([[cohr]], [[axti]])
- **Structural demand**: May smooth traditional cycles
