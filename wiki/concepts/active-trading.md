# Active Trading

Short-to-medium term trading approach using technical analysis and market timing to generate profits from price movements.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Price action tells the story**: Charts reflect all known information
2. **Risk management first**: Preserve capital to stay in the game
3. **Cut losses, let winners run**: Asymmetric risk/reward targeting
4. **Discipline over emotion**: Follow your system, not your feelings
5. **Position sizing is survival**: Never risk too much on one trade

---

## Trading Timeframes

| Style | Holding Period | Analysis Focus |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Day trading** | Minutes to hours | Intraday charts, volume |
| **Swing trading** | Days to weeks | Daily/weekly charts |
| **Position trading** | Weeks to months | Weekly/monthly charts |

### Technical Analysis

### Risk Management
- [[position-sizing-risk-management]]: 2% rule, stop-losses
- Risk/reward ratios (minimum 2:1 recommended)
- Portfolio heat management

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Overtrading**: Taking low-quality setups
- **No stop-loss**: Hoping losers will recover
- **Oversizing**: Risking too much on "sure things"
- **Revenge trading**: Trying to immediately recover losses
- **Ignoring the trend**: Fighting market direction

---

## Tools Required

- Charting platform (TradingView, ThinkOrSwim)
- Real-time quotes
- Trading journal (track every trade)
- Risk calculator

---

## Contrast with [[long-term-investing]]

Active trading requires significantly more:
- Time commitment (daily monitoring)
- Emotional discipline
- Transaction costs (commissions, slippage)
- Tax efficiency planning (short-term capital gains)
