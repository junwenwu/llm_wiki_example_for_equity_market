# Long-Term Investing

A buy-and-hold investment approach focused on wealth accumulation over years or decades rather than short-term trading profits.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Time in market > timing the market**: Consistent investing beats trying to predict tops/bottoms
2. **Compound growth**: Reinvested returns generate exponential growth over time
3. **Cost minimization**: Low fees compound into significant savings
4. **Diversification**: Spread risk across asset classes and geographies
5. **Emotional discipline**: Avoid panic selling during market downturns

## Time Horizons

- **Short-term** (< 3 years): Savings accounts, CDs, short-term bonds
- **Medium-term** (3-10 years): Balanced funds, bond allocation
- **Long-term** (10+ years): Equity-heavy, can tolerate volatility

---

## Psychological Challenges

- **FOMO**: Fear of missing out on hot stocks
- **Loss aversion**: Panic selling in downturns
- **Recency bias**: Assuming recent trends continue
- **Overconfidence**: Believing you can beat the market

---

## Contrast with [[active-trading]]

| Long-Term Investing | Active Trading |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Years/decades holding period | Days to months |
| Fundamental focus | Technical focus |
| Lower fees/taxes | Higher transaction costs |
| Less time-intensive | Requires active monitoring |
