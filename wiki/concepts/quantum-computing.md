# Quantum Computing

Emerging computing paradigm using quantum mechanical phenomena (superposition, entanglement) to solve problems intractable for classical computers.

---

## Key Approaches

### Quantum Annealing

Specialized for optimization problems. Uses quantum effects to find lowest-energy solutions.

**Strengths:**
- Available commercially today
- Thousands of qubits
- Practical for specific optimization use cases

**Limitations:**
- Not universal (can't run all algorithms)
- Limited to certain problem types

**Key Player:** [[qbts]] (D-Wave) - First commercial quantum computer company

### Gate-Based (Universal)

General-purpose quantum computing using quantum logic gates.

**Strengths:**
- Universal computation capability
- Can run any quantum algorithm (Shor's, Grover's)
- Theoretical advantage over classical computers

**Limitations:**
- Fewer stable qubits currently
- High error rates require error correction
- Years/decades from practical scale

**Key Players:** IBM, Google, IonQ, Rigetti

## Investment Considerations

### Bull Case
- First-mover advantage in emerging market
- Government/defense contracts (quantum-safe cryptography)
- Some optimization problems solved better today
- Dual-platform strategies hedge technical risk

### Bear Case
- Very early stage, unclear commercial viability
- Path to profitability may take 5-10+ years
- Gate-based competitors have strong backing
- Quantum winter risk if progress stalls
