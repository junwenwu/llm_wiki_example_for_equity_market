## What Is Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)?

Investing can be challenging. Even experienced investors who try to time the market can come up short. Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) can remove the uncertainty of market timing by adhering to a fixed investment schedule. This strategy also supports an investor's effort to invest regularly.

DCA involves investing the same amount of money in a target security at regular intervals over a certain period, regardless of price. Using DCA may lower an investor's average cost per share and reduce the impact of volatility on their portfolios. In effect, this strategy eliminates the effort required when attempting to time the market to buy at the best prices.

### Key Takeaways

- Dollar-cost averaging can reduce the overall impact of price volatility and lower the average cost per share.
- By buying regularly in up and down markets, investors buy more shares at lower prices and fewer shares at higher prices.
- DCA aims to prevent a poorly timed lump-sum investment at a potentially higher price.
- Novice and experienced investors can both benefit from DCA.

**Also known as**: the constant dollar plan.

## How Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) Works

DCA is a simple tool that an investor can use to save and build wealth over the long term. It's also a way for an investor to ignore short-term volatility in the broader markets.

### 401(k) Example

A prime example of long-term DCA is its use in 401(k) plans, in which employees invest regularly regardless of the price of the investment.

With a 401(k) plan:
- Employees can choose how much they want to contribute
- They select investments offered by the plan in which to invest
- Investments are made automatically every pay period
- Depending on the markets, employees may see a larger or smaller number of securities added to their accounts

### Other Uses

DCA can also be used outside of 401(k) plans:
- Buy mutual or index fund shares regularly in a traditional IRA
- Invest in a taxable brokerage account
- Trade exchange-traded funds (ETFs)
- Use dividend reinvestment plans (DRIPs)

---

## Benefits of DCA

1. **Lower average cost**: It can lower the average amount you spend on investments.
2. **Regular investing habit**: It reinforces the practice of investing regularly to build wealth over time.
3. **Automatic**: It's automatic and can take concerns about when to invest out of your hands.
4. **Avoids bad timing**: It removes the pitfalls of market timing, such as buying only when prices have already risen.
5. **Ready for opportunities**: It can ensure that you're already in the market and ready to buy when events send prices higher.
6. **Removes emotion**: It takes emotion out of your investing and prevents you from potentially damaging your portfolio's returns.

## Who Should Use DCA?

Any investor can use DCA as an investment strategy. DCA may be especially useful to:
- **New investors** who don't have the experience or expertise to judge the most opportune moments to buy
- **Long-term investors** who are committed to investing regularly but don't have the time or inclination to watch the market and time their orders

**Note**: DCA isn't for everyone. It isn't necessarily appropriate for those investing when prices are trending steadily in one direction or the other.

## Example of DCA

### Scenario

Jordan works at ABC Corp. and has a 401(k) plan. They receive a paycheck of $1,000 every two weeks. Jordan decides to allocate 10% ($100) of their pay to their employer's plan every pay period.

They contribute:
- 50% to a large-cap mutual fund
- 50% to an S&P 500 index fund

Every two weeks, $50 goes to each fund, regardless of the fund's price.

### Results Over 10 Pay Periods

| Pay Period | Fund Price | Shares Bought | Total Shares |
|------------|------------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | $10.00 | 5.00 | 5.00 |
| 2 | $11.00 | 4.55 | 9.55 |
| 3 | $10.50 | 4.76 | 14.31 |
| 4 | $11.00 | 4.55 | 18.86 |
| 5 | $9.50 | 5.26 | 24.12 |
| 6 | $9.00 | 5.56 | 29.68 |
| 7 | $10.00 | 5.00 | 34.68 |
| 8 | $10.50 | 4.76 | 39.44 |
| 9 | $11.50 | 4.35 | 43.79 |
| 10 | $12.00 | 4.17 | 47.96 |

**DCA Results:**
- Total invested: $500
- Total shares: 47.71
- Average price paid: $10.48 ($500 ÷ 47.71)

**If Jordan Spent One Lump Sum (at $11 in period 4):**
- Shares purchased: 45.45 ($500 ÷ $11)

**Outcome**: By using DCA, Jordan got **more shares (47.71 vs 45.45)** at a **lower average price ($10.48 vs $11.00)**.

## Special Considerations

**Warning**: DCA can't protect investors against the risk of declining market prices. However, like the outlook of many long-term investors, this strategy assumes that prices (though they may drop at times) will ultimately rise.

**Caution for individual stocks**: Using this strategy to buy an individual stock without researching a company's details could prove detrimental. An investor might continue to buy more stock when they would otherwise stop buying or exit the position.

**Recommendation**: For less-informed investors, the strategy is far less risky when used to buy index funds rather than individual stocks.

---

## When DCA Works Best

DCA works well when you buy an investment over a specific period of time when the price fluctuates up and down.

**Scenarios where DCA may not be optimal:**
- If the price rises continuously, those using DCA end up buying fewer shares (lump sum would have been better)
- If it declines continuously, they may continue buying when they should be on the sidelines

---

## How Often Should You Invest With DCA?

The frequency depends on your:
- Investment horizon
- Outlook on the market
- Experience with investing

**Common approach**: Apply some of every paycheck to the regular purchases.

---

## The Bottom Line

Dollar-cost averaging is a straightforward way of investing that reduces the emotional stress of trying to invest at the right time. By sticking to a fixed investment schedule and amount, you'll naturally buy more shares when the price is low vs. high.

DCA may not be a perfect investment strategy, as it doesn't protect against market risk. However, it allows investors to:
- Stick with regular investing
- Build up a portfolio over time
- Decrease the anxiety of trying to time the market
