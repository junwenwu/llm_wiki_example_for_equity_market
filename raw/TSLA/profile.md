## Business Description

Tesla, Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, leases, and sells electric vehicles, and energy generation and storage systems in the United States, China, and internationally. The company operates in two segments: Automotive; and Energy Generation and Storage.

The Automotive segment offers electric vehicles, sells automotive regulatory credits, and provides non-warranty maintenance services, collision repair, automotive insurance services, part sales, and retail merchandise. Tesla offers sedans and sport utility vehicles through direct and used vehicle sales, a network of Tesla Superchargers, and in-app upgrades. The company provides purchase financing and leasing services, services for electric vehicles through company-owned service locations and Tesla mobile service technicians, and vehicle limited warranties and extended service plans.

The Energy Generation and Storage segment engages in the design, manufacture, installation, sale, and leasing of solar energy generation and energy storage products, and related services to residential, commercial, and industrial customers and utilities through its website, stores, galleries, and network of channel partners.

## Market Position

Tesla is the world's leading electric vehicle manufacturer and a pioneer in sustainable energy solutions. The company has established a dominant position in the premium EV market and continues to expand into mass-market segments. Tesla operates a vertically integrated business model with in-house manufacturing, direct sales, and its proprietary Supercharger network. The company is heavily investing in AI for autonomous driving, robotics, and energy storage solutions.
