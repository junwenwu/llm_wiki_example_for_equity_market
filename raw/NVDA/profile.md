## Business Description

NVIDIA Corporation operates as a data center scale AI infrastructure company worldwide. The company operates through two segments: Compute & Networking, and Graphics.

The Compute & Networking segment provides data center accelerated computing and networking platforms, artificial intelligence solutions and software, and automotive platforms including autonomous and electric vehicle solutions with software. The Graphics segment offers GeForce GPUs for gaming and PCs, and Quadro/NVIDIA RTX GPUs for enterprise workstation graphics.

The company's products serve gaming, professional visualization, data center, and automotive markets. NVIDIA sells its products to original equipment manufacturers, original device manufacturers, system integrators and distributors, independent software vendors, cloud service providers, add-in board manufacturers, automotive manufacturers and tier-1 automotive suppliers.

## Key Products & Services

- **Data Center GPUs**: AI accelerators and compute platforms
- **GeForce**: Consumer gaming GPUs
- **Quadro/RTX**: Professional workstation graphics
- **DRIVE**: Autonomous vehicle platforms
- **Networking**: Data center networking solutions (Mellanox)
- **AI Software**: CUDA, AI frameworks, enterprise AI software

## Market Position

NVIDIA is the dominant leader in AI/ML accelerators and GPU computing. The company holds approximately 80%+ market share in data center AI training chips. Key competitive advantages include:

- Industry-leading CUDA software ecosystem
- First-mover advantage in AI infrastructure
- Full-stack solutions from chips to software
- Strong partnerships with all major cloud providers
- Continuous innovation in chip architecture (Blackwell, Rubin)
