## Key Takeaways

### Customer Metrics
- ~296,000 paying customers (record net addition of ~30,000 sequentially)
- 4,009 large customers (>$100K annually), up 23% Y/Y
- Large customers now contribute 73% of revenue (up from 67% last year)
- Record number of $1M+ and $5M+ customers for fourth consecutive quarter
- No customer bigger than 2% of revenue

### Guidance
**Q4 2025:**
- Revenue: $588.5M - $589.5M (+28% Y/Y)
- Operating Income: $83M - $84M
- Diluted EPS: $0.27

**Full Year 2025:**
- Revenue: $2.104B - $2.143B (+28% Y/Y)
- Operating Income: $297M - $298M
- Diluted EPS: $0.91

### Long-term Targets
- Expect $3 billion annualized revenue run rate in 2026
- $5 billion annualized revenue goal by 2028

## Strategic Highlights

### Go-to-Market Transformation
- Salesforce capacity growth at fastest pace in 2+ years
- Sales productivity increased Y/Y for 7th consecutive quarter
- Partner-initiated bookings doubled Y/Y
- Gross retention increased Y/Y and Q/Q
- Pool of funds deals now "low double digits" of total ACV

### Key Customer Wins
1. **Global Digital Media Platform:** $22.8M, 3-year pool of funds (comeback from 2016 loss)
2. **European Tech Company:** $34.3M, 5-year contract (front-end redesign on Workers)
3. **U.S. Cabinet-Level Agency:** >$20M, 2-year FedRAMP portfolio (displacing 12+ legacy solutions)
4. **Fortune 500 FinTech:** $16.1M, 2-year pool of funds
5. **European Pharma:** $12.4M, 3-year contract
6. **Media Platform:** $15M, 3-year for Workers (eliminating egress fees, >30% TCO reduction)

### Leadership Change
- CJ Desai (President/COO) departing to become CEO of another public tech company
- Legacy: customer-first focus, processes, discipline, leadership bench

## CEO Matthew Prince Key Themes

### On AI and the Future Internet
"I don't think people yet appreciate how AI is another massive information consumption platform shift. Just as we moved from consuming information via a browser on a desktop to social media, then to apps on mobile devices, AI is another information consumption platform shift."

"With AI, for the first time in a long time, the fundamental business model is going to change. Human eyeball traffic is unlikely to be the currency of the Internet's future."

"We estimate 80% of the leading AI companies already rely on us. A huge percentage of the Internet sits behind us. The agents of the future will inherently have to pass through our network and abide by its rules."

### On Workers Platform
"Our Workers Developer platform continues to deliver outsized growth, with the world's most innovative companies increasingly adopting workers for running AI inference tasks as well as building AI agents and full-stack applications."

### On Pool of Funds
"I think we saw downward pressure on dollar-based net retention as we rolled out pool of funds. As those pools are now getting consumed, you can see our dollar-based net retention is ticking back up... These are an indication of customers trusting us as a strategic vendor, making larger, bigger bets on us."

### On Competition
"The primary competition for inference workloads continues to be the hyperscalers... We're having to teach them that there is a different way that's out there. But the primary competition still comes from the hyperscalers."

### On Quantum Security
"We have rolled [post-quantum cryptography] out across our entire network for every customer, whether they pay us or not, because we believe that everybody should have the foundational levels of the best of security at no cost."

## CFO Thomas Seifert Key Points

### On Revenue Drivers
"The RPO cost points to primarily two drivers: the customer quality and the platform expansion. We are seeing exceptional strengths with our large customer cohorts, specifically those that spend more than $1 million or $5 million with us."

### On CapEx
"Network CapEx represented 14% of revenue in the third quarter. We expect Network CapEx to be approximately 13% of revenue for the full year 2025."

### On Supply Chain
"The supply chain within Cloudflare is so optimized to a large degree because we use off-the-shelf equipment and parts that we can deploy hardware, especially in tier-one cities, and generate revenue even before we start to pay for the equipment."

## Product & Strategy Highlights

### NetDollar Initiative
- Digital currency project for agent-to-agent commerce
- Designed for regulatory compliance
- Partnerships with Coinbase (x402), Visa, Mastercard, American Express
- Not going all-in on one approach - supporting multiple payment protocols

### AI Gateway
- Helping media companies figure out new business models
- Expanding beyond media to banks, brands, small businesses
- Focus on "pay-per-crawl" opportunities

### Oracle Partnership
- Cloudflare products natively available within Oracle's OCI platform
- Covering hybrid, multi-cloud, and OCI-hosted workloads

### SASE Strategy
- Shifting to partner-first approach
- 95% of competitor (Netskope, Zscaler) sales through channel
- "Significant uptick in partner-led opportunities"

## Industry Glossary

- **Workers Developer Platform:** Serverless environment for apps, AI inference, and programmable network logic at the edge
- **Pool of Funds Contract:** Prepaid credits for flexible cross-product consumption
- **SASE:** Secure Access Service Edge - cloud-delivered security and networking services
- **AI Gateway:** Product to manage, gate, and monetize data access from AI agents
- **NetDollar:** Digital currency initiative for agent-to-agent commerce
- **Magic Transit:** DDoS protection and secure traffic flow for enterprise infrastructure
