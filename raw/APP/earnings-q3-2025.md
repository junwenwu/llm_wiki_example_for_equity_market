## Key Takeaways

### S&P 500 Inclusion
- Company was formally added to the S&P 500
- Management identified as operational milestone reflecting enhanced market visibility and institutional focus

### Revenue Performance
- $1.405 billion revenue, up 68% Y/Y
- Growth primarily attributed to core gaming model updates

### Profitability
- Adjusted EBITDA of $1.158 billion at 82% margin
- Up 1% sequentially from Q2 2025 driven by operating leverage and lower FX costs
- Quarter-over-quarter flow through to adjusted EBITDA was 95%

### Free Cash Flow
- $1.049 billion, up 92% Y/Y
- Margin improved sequentially (no semiannual cash interest on debt this period)

### Capital Allocation
- Repurchased ~1.3 million shares for $571 million
- Reduced weighted average diluted shares from 346M (Q4 2024) to 341M
- Board increased share repurchase authorization by $3.2 billion

### Q4 2025 Guidance
- Revenue: $1.57B - $1.60B (12%-14% sequential growth)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $1.29B - $1.32B
- Target margin: 82%-83%

## Self-Service Platform Launch (October 1)

**Key Points:**
- Launched without major bugs or customer complaints
- Early week-over-week spend growth of ~50%
- CEO Foroughi: "It is too soon to be significant"
- Referral-based onboarding remains gated for quality control
- New advertisers are "comparable in mix" to last year's pilot group
- Broad set of shopping categories being represented

**2026 Priorities:**
1. Continue tuning onboarding flows
2. Ramp more AI agents into workflow
3. Test generative AI-based ad creatives
4. Test paid marketing to promote Axon Ads platform
5. Open platform broadly beyond referral basis once quality standards met

## International Expansion

- Opened international traffic for web/shop advertisers (excluding EU) ahead of schedule
- EU represents low teens percentage of business
- GDPR build-out not currently prioritized
- English-speaking markets (Canada, Australia, New Zealand) leading early expansion
- Localization for Japan and Korea planned as platform scales

---

## AI Investment & Automation

**Current State:**
- AI bots automatically checking ad quality and website quality
- LLM-powered tools in dashboard for customer self-support
- Limited customer support tickets despite new onboarding

**Future Plans:**
- Generative AI-based creative testing expected "in a matter of weeks or months"
- CEO: "If we can move to mostly automated creative generation, we believe user response rates to more customized ads on our platform will materially improve"
- Average ad viewership: 35 seconds (vs. 7 seconds on social)

## CEO Adam Foroughi Key Quotes

### On S&P 500 Inclusion:
"A huge milestone for our company and a strong acknowledgment of what we built. It is a privilege we do not take lightly. It also means we now carry the expectations of a much broader set of investors."

### On Self-Service Platform:
"We are already seeing spend from these self-service advertisers grow, something I was personally monitoring closely, around roughly 50% week over week. It is too soon to be significant."

### On Conversion Rate Improvement:
"The biggest lever for growth on our business, given we report on a net revenue basis, is increasing the conversion rate. That happens from model enhancements and advertiser density expanding."

### On Supply Constraints:
"We are not supply constrained today. We are demand constrained. If we do our job right and bring on a lot of advertisers, it serves us well because it serves them well."

### On Generative AI Creative:
"I am hoping in a matter of weeks or months, be able to test generative AI-based creative that we create with a little help... That in itself can really explode the count of ads on the platform."

### On Gaming vs E-commerce Balance:
"I am really confident that not gonna squeeze anyone in our platform. We are probably gonna have expansion across the board as we add more demand density and get more data into the system."

## CFO Matthew Stumpf Key Points

### On Guidance Philosophy:
"We guide to what we know. We do not try to estimate for something that is unpredictable. There is no incremental assumption built into the guide for onboarding of incremental customers."

### On CapEx:
"We are pay as you go... We do not try to really overinvest ahead of revenue. We want to make sure really disciplined. And that is completely aligned with our culture where we want to be cost disciplined in every aspect of the business."

## Q&A Highlights

### On Advertiser Onboarding Quality (James Heaney)
New advertisers via referral are comparable in mix and size to last year's pilot group - not "local dry cleaners" but substantial businesses across broad shopping categories.

### On Conversion Rates (Omar Dessouky)
Three drivers for conversion rate improvement:
1. Model enhancements (continuous)
2. Advertiser density expanding (better personalization)
3. Generative AI-based creative (automated, customized ads)

### On Direct Payments Impact (Alec Brondolo)
- Not contributing much yet
- If app store commissions decline from 30% to 15%, could provide ~20% lift in LTV for in-app purchasing games
- Impact expected to build over quarters

### On Platform Quality Standards (Matthew Cost)
Focus areas before general availability:
- Conversion funnel optimization
- Client communications and emails
- Dashboard tooling and AI bots
- CEO: "If we are not ready yet today, we are gonna take our time to get there"

## Industry Glossary

- **MAX:** AppLovin's supply-side platform enabling in-app bidding for mobile app advertising inventory
- **Axon Ads:** AppLovin's self-service advertising platform for onboarding advertisers
- **GDPR:** General Data Protection Regulation (EU data privacy law)
- **LTV:** Lifetime Value - total net profit from customer relationship
- **Referral Program:** Mechanism for admitting new advertisers through invitations for quality control
