# Semiconductor Industry Cycle Guide

Understanding the cyclical nature of the semiconductor industry for better investment timing.

---

## Overview of the Semiconductor Cycle

The semiconductor industry is highly cyclical, driven by:
- **Supply/demand imbalances** — Capacity takes 2-3 years to build
- **Inventory cycles** — Customers over-order in shortages, under-order in gluts
- **End market demand** — PCs, smartphones, data centers, automotive
- **Technology transitions** — New nodes drive upgrade cycles

### Typical Cycle Length
- **Full cycle**: 3-5 years (peak to peak)
- **Downturn**: 12-18 months
- **Recovery**: 12-24 months
- **Expansion**: 12-24 months

## Cycle Phases & Investment Strategy

### Phase 1: Downturn (Correction)

**Characteristics:**
- Demand falling, inventory building
- Price cuts, margin compression
- Layoffs, capex cuts announced
- Negative earnings revisions
- Stocks down 30-50% from peak

**What to Watch:**
- Inventory levels peaking
- Order cancellations
- Utilization rates declining
- ASPs (Average Selling Prices) falling

**Strategy:**
- Build watchlist
- Start small positions in quality names
- Focus on strong balance sheets

### Phase 2: Trough (Bottom)

**Characteristics:**
- Inventories starting to normalize
- Demand stabilizing at low levels
- Capex cuts completed
- Sentiment extremely negative
- Valuations compressed

**What to Watch:**
- Inventory days improving
- Book-to-bill turning positive
- Lead times extending
- Management tone improving

**Strategy:**
- Accumulate aggressively
- Best risk/reward in the cycle
- Focus on market share gainers

### Phase 3: Recovery (Expansion)

**Characteristics:**
- Demand recovering
- Pricing stabilizing/improving
- Utilization rising
- Positive earnings revisions
- Stocks rallying

**What to Watch:**
- Revenue acceleration
- Margin expansion
- Capex increases announced
- Hiring resuming

**Strategy:**
- Hold winners, add on pullbacks
- Rotate to higher-beta names
- Watch for overheating signs

### Phase 4: Peak (Overheating)

**Characteristics:**
- Demand exceeding supply
- Double-ordering by customers
- Record margins
- Euphoric sentiment
- Stretched valuations

**What to Watch:**
- Inventory build at customers
- Lead times extremely long
- Capacity additions announced
- "Supercycle" narratives

**Strategy:**
- Take profits
- Reduce position sizes
- Avoid chasing momentum
- Raise cash for next downturn

## Investment Frameworks

### Quality vs Cyclicality Matrix

| | High Quality | Lower Quality |
|---|-------------|---------------|
| **High Cyclicality** | MU (when timed right) | Commodity memory |
| **Low Cyclicality** | NVDA, AVGO, ASML | Mature analog |

### Cycle Timing Approach

1. **Buy on downturn** — When sentiment is worst
2. **Hold through recovery** — Let earnings catch up
3. **Trim at peak** — When "supercycle" talk emerges
4. **Raise cash** — Prepare for next downturn

### Structural Growth Approach

1. **Focus on secular winners** — NVDA, ASML, TSM
2. **Ignore cycle timing** — Dollar-cost average
3. **Accept volatility** — 30-50% drawdowns normal
4. **Hold for technology transitions** — AI, auto, etc.

## The Bottom Line

The semiconductor industry offers exceptional returns for investors who understand the cycle. Key principles:

1. **The cycle always turns** — Both directions
2. **Sentiment extremes mark turning points** — Fear at bottoms, greed at tops
3. **Inventory is the key metric** — Watch it obsessively
4. **Structural winners compound through cycles** — Quality matters
5. **Patience is rewarded** — Best returns come from buying fear

The AI boom has created a potential "supercycle" in some segments (GPUs, HBM, networking), but the industry's cyclical nature means downturns will still occur. Position sizing and cycle awareness remain critical.
