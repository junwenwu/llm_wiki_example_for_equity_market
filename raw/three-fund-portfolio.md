## What Is a Three-Fund Portfolio?

A three-fund portfolio is a simple, diversified investment strategy that uses just three index funds to cover the entire market. Popularized by the Bogleheads community (followers of Vanguard founder John Bogle), this approach emphasizes simplicity, low costs, and broad diversification.

### The Three Funds

1. **U.S. Total Stock Market Index Fund** — Covers all U.S. stocks (large, mid, small cap)
2. **International Total Stock Market Index Fund** — Covers stocks from developed and emerging markets outside the U.S.
3. **U.S. Total Bond Market Index Fund** — Covers the entire U.S. investment-grade bond market

---

## Why Three Funds?

### Simplicity
- Easy to understand and manage
- Minimal rebalancing required
- No need to pick individual stocks or time the market

### Diversification
- Exposure to thousands of stocks and bonds
- Geographic diversification (U.S. and international)
- Asset class diversification (stocks and bonds)

### Low Cost
- Index funds have the lowest expense ratios
- No sales loads or commissions
- Tax-efficient due to low turnover

### Philosophy
John Bogle's key insight: "Don't look for the needle in the haystack. Just buy the haystack!"

## Asset Allocation by Age

The bond allocation typically increases as you approach retirement. Common rules of thumb:

### Rule: "Your Age in Bonds"
- Age 25: 25% bonds, 75% stocks
- Age 40: 40% bonds, 60% stocks
- Age 60: 60% bonds, 40% stocks

### Modified Rule: "Age Minus 10 or 20 in Bonds"
More aggressive approach for longer life expectancy:
- Age 25: 5-15% bonds, 85-95% stocks
- Age 40: 20-30% bonds, 70-80% stocks
- Age 60: 40-50% bonds, 50-60% stocks

---

## Stock Allocation: U.S. vs. International

Common approaches for the stock portion:

| Approach | U.S. Stocks | International Stocks | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Market-weight | ~60% | ~40% | Reflects global market cap |
| U.S. tilt | 70-80% | 20-30% | Home country preference |
| U.S. heavy | 80-100% | 0-20% | U.S. outperformance belief |

**Bogleheads recommendation**: Hold international stocks at market weight (~40%) or with slight U.S. tilt (60-70% U.S.)

## How to Implement

### Step 1: Choose Your Brokerage
Select a low-cost brokerage (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab) based on:
- Fund availability
- Expense ratios
- Account minimums
- User interface

### Step 2: Determine Your Asset Allocation
Consider:
- Your age
- Risk tolerance
- Time horizon
- Other income sources (pension, Social Security)

### Step 3: Set Up Automatic Investments
- Enable automatic contributions
- Use dollar-cost averaging
- Reinvest dividends automatically

### Step 4: Rebalance Annually
- Check allocations once a year
- Rebalance if any fund drifts more than 5% from target
- Use new contributions to rebalance when possible (avoids taxes in taxable accounts)

## Advantages of Three-Fund Portfolio

1. **Simplicity**: Easy to understand and implement
2. **Diversification**: Thousands of securities across global markets
3. **Low cost**: Minimal expense ratios (often under 0.10%)
4. **Tax efficiency**: Low turnover means fewer capital gains distributions
5. **No manager risk**: Index funds don't depend on any manager's skill
6. **Broad market exposure**: Captures market returns without stock picking
7. **Easy rebalancing**: Only three funds to track and adjust

---

## Potential Drawbacks

1. **No outperformance potential**: You won't beat the market
2. **Full market exposure in downturns**: No defensive positioning
3. **Simplification may not fit everyone**: Some prefer more granular control
4. **Currency risk**: International funds expose you to exchange rate fluctuations

---

## The Bottom Line

The three-fund portfolio is a proven, time-tested strategy that works for most investors. By keeping costs low, staying diversified, and investing consistently, you can build wealth over time without the complexity of active management.

As John Bogle said: "The stock market is a giant distraction to the business of investing."
