# Earnings Report Analysis Guide

A comprehensive framework for analyzing quarterly earnings reports and calls.

---

## What Is an Earnings Report?

Public companies report financial results quarterly (10-Q) and annually (10-K). The earnings release typically includes:

1. **Press Release** — Summary of results, highlights, guidance
2. **Financial Statements** — Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow
3. **Earnings Call** — Management discussion, analyst Q&A
4. **Presentation Deck** — Visual summary of key metrics

---

## Key Metrics to Track

### Revenue Metrics

| Metric | What It Tells You | Watch For |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| **Total Revenue** | Top-line growth | Beat/miss vs consensus |
| **Revenue Growth YoY** | Year-over-year comparison | Acceleration or deceleration |
| **Revenue Growth QoQ** | Sequential comparison | Seasonality effects |
| **Organic Growth** | Growth excluding M&A | True business momentum |
| **Revenue by Segment** | Business mix | Which segments are driving growth |
| **Geographic Mix** | Regional exposure | Currency, geopolitical risks |

### Profitability Metrics

| Metric | Formula | What It Tells You |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | Pricing power, cost control |
| **Operating Margin** | Operating Income / Revenue | Core business profitability |
| **Net Margin** | Net Income / Revenue | Bottom-line efficiency |
| **EBITDA Margin** | EBITDA / Revenue | Cash generation ability |
| **EPS (Earnings Per Share)** | Net Income / Shares Outstanding | Per-share profitability |
| **Adjusted EPS** | Excludes one-time items | Comparable earnings |

### Cash Flow Metrics

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| **Operating Cash Flow (OCF)** | Cash from core operations |
| **Free Cash Flow (FCF)** | OCF minus CapEx—available for dividends, buybacks, debt |
| **CapEx** | Investment in growth |
| **FCF Conversion** | FCF / Net Income—quality of earnings |

## The Beat/Miss Framework

### Revenue
- **Beat**: Actual > Consensus estimate
- **Miss**: Actual < Consensus estimate
- **In-line**: Within ~1% of estimate

### EPS
- **Beat**: Actual > Consensus estimate
- **Miss**: Actual < Consensus estimate
- **Whisper number**: Unofficial higher expectation (beat consensus but miss whisper = stock drops)

### Why Stocks Drop on "Beats"

1. **Guidance weak** — Future outlook matters more than past
2. **Quality of beat** — One-time items, tax benefits
3. **Whisper miss** — Street expected more
4. **Segment weakness** — Key growth driver disappointed
5. **Margin compression** — Revenue beat but margins contracted

## Guidance Analysis

### Types of Guidance

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Point estimate** | Single number (e.g., "$25B revenue") |
| **Range** | Low to high (e.g., "$24.5B to $25.5B") |
| **Qualitative** | Directional language ("expect modest growth") |
| **Withdrawn** | No guidance (uncertainty signal) |

### Guidance Interpretation

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Raised guidance** | Business better than expected—bullish |
| **Maintained guidance** | On track, no surprises |
| **Lowered guidance** | Headwinds emerging—bearish |
| **Narrowed range** | More visibility, confidence increasing |
| **Widened range** | Uncertainty increasing |
| **Above/below consensus** | Key comparison point |

### What to Compare

- **Q+1 guidance vs consensus** — Next quarter expectations
- **Full-year guidance vs consensus** — Annual trajectory
- **Long-term targets** — Are they reaffirming or pulling back?

## Earnings Call Anatomy

### Structure (Typical)

1. **Safe Harbor Statement** — Legal disclaimer (~2 min)
2. **CEO Remarks** — Strategic overview, highlights (~10 min)
3. **CFO Remarks** — Financial details, guidance (~10 min)
4. **Q&A Session** — Analyst questions (~30-45 min)

### What to Listen For

#### Tone & Language
| Bullish Signals | Bearish Signals |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "Exceeded expectations" | "Challenging environment" |
| "Strong momentum" | "Headwinds" |
| "Accelerating" | "Moderating" |
| "Record results" | "Below expectations" |
| "Confident in outlook" | "Cautious near-term" |

#### Key Topics
- **Demand trends** — Order book, pipeline, backlog
- **Pricing** — ASP trends, ability to raise prices
- **Competition** — Market share, competitive dynamics
- **Macro** — Economic sensitivity, currency impacts
- **Capital allocation** — Buybacks, dividends, M&A plans
- **Hiring** — Growth investments vs cost cuts

## Pre-Earnings Checklist

### Before the Report
- [ ] Note consensus estimates (Revenue, EPS)
- [ ] Check whisper numbers if available
- [ ] Review prior quarter's guidance
- [ ] Note key questions/concerns from prior call
- [ ] Check options implied move

### During/After the Report
- [ ] Compare actual vs estimates (beat/miss)
- [ ] Read guidance section first
- [ ] Note segment performance
- [ ] Check gross/operating margins
- [ ] Listen to call for tone
- [ ] Track analyst questions (what's concerning them?)

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Focusing Only on Headline Numbers
The beat/miss doesn't tell the full story. Always check:
- Quality of revenue (one-time vs recurring)
- Margin trends
- Guidance trajectory

### 2. Ignoring the Call
Management tone, analyst questions, and forward commentary often matter more than the numbers.

### 3. Reacting Immediately
After-hours moves often reverse. Wait for full information before making decisions.

### 4. Comparing Apples to Oranges
- **GAAP vs Non-GAAP** — Know which EPS is being reported
- **Sequential vs YoY** — Different stories
- **Reported vs Constant Currency** — FX can distort trends

## The Bottom Line

Earnings analysis is about understanding the business trajectory, not just comparing numbers to estimates. Focus on:

1. **Revenue quality and sustainability**
2. **Margin trends and drivers**
3. **Management's forward outlook**
4. **What the market is focused on (the narrative)**

The best investors read beyond the headlines and understand what the numbers mean for the business long-term.
